The starfield's back — as characters, the way a terminal draws it.

The nebula and its starfield return, restored to what they always were under the web glow: plain cells (. · * : #) with a violet/cyan core and a scatter of gold, blue, and rose stars, and the Braille wordmark (2×4 sub-dots per cell) floating inside it under a soft violet→cyan→green nebula gradient. The ambient pulse is honest — no alpha fade exists in a terminal, so a few glyph cells flare (not the whole word) and the stars twinkle, each stepping through a small truecolor ramp on one timer (what Textual would do). A cell is the finest unit a terminal can colour — one glyph, one colour — so flaring a handful of characters is as granular as it gets. Toggle it off, and it goes static under reduced-motion. ↑/↓ or j/k to move, / for the palette, esc to close.
